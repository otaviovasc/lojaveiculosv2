# Frontend experience audit — July 2026

This is the implementation backlog produced from a read-only `agy` review of
the current seeded application, source inspection, and Playwright captures at
`1440x900` and `390x844`. It covers distinct product surfaces rather than route
aliases. Legitimate empty, disconnected, loading, restricted, and reduced-motion
states are evaluated as states; they are not treated as data defects.

The visual direction is defined by [`ui-ux-visual-quality.md`](ui-ux-visual-quality.md).
The dashboard's automotive composition, purposeful color, branded watermark,
and information density remain the quality bar. The shell hierarchy is one of
the strongest parts of the newer work: its surface follows the selected theme,
using warm light panels in light mode and graphite in dark mode.

## Priority order

1. Protect trust: never show fabricated metrics, contradictory billing states,
   incorrect status colors/icons, mismatched vehicle media, third-party contact
   marks, or internal storage/debug values.
2. Make every factual value responsive: currency, counts, filters, tab rails,
   toolbars, dialogs, PDFs, and code samples must fit a 390px viewport without
   clipping or hidden actions.
3. Replace repeated oversized page titles with compact domain command decks,
   controls, or tabs. Do not flatten the branded canvas.
4. Standardize semantic color: green complete/available, gold pending/reserved,
   blue informational/automatic/sold, red primary/blocking/destructive.
5. Validate the actual flows and generated bytes, not only list-page snapshots.

## Generated artifacts and exports

- PDF preview must render the exact stored/downloaded bytes, fit its stage at
  every viewport, expose localized loading/error recovery, include metadata,
  and avoid orphan headings.
- Contracts, receipts, test-drive terms, proposals, and vehicle sheets require
  human labels, store identity, dates, parties, monetary values, signatures,
  page numbering, and no ids/storage keys/provider enums.
- A payment receipt is incomplete without the paid amount and payment context.
- CSV exports require localized headers, stable encoding, active-filter metadata,
  and formula-injection protection.
- Vehicle photo ZIPs require deterministic filenames, ordering, progress,
  cancellation/error feedback, and no unrelated tenant media.
- Public API Docs, OpenAPI, `llms.txt`, manifest, AI-tool definitions, and curl
  samples must agree on auth, routes, schemas, and scopes.
- Storefront uploads/crops require clean source provenance; seeded media may not
  carry external dealership contact marks.

## Shared primitives to converge on

- `DomainCommandDeck`: compact title/context/period/actions without a repeated
  oversized page hero.
- `ResponsiveKpiGrid`: four columns on desktop, two-by-two on mobile, with
  overflow-safe values and semantic tones.
- `SemanticStatus`: one precedence model for success, info, pending, reconnect,
  blocked, and destructive states.
- `VehicleMediaFallback`: compact branded missing-media state shared by
  inventory, sales, CRM, and storefront administration.
- `OverflowTabRail`: keyboard-accessible horizontal tabs with edge fades and
  active-tab visibility.
- `ArtifactViewer`: same-byte PDF/image preview with callback-ref sizing,
  localized recovery, zoom/download controls, and responsive thumbnails.
- `ResponsiveDataSurface`: desktop table plus mobile cards from one data model,
  with explicit sort/filter/action parity.

## Motion policy

Anime.js may provide scoped, interruptible entry and state-transition motion.
Motion must finish within the visual contract, clean up on unmount, and honor
`prefers-reduced-motion`. Operational totals render their factual value
immediately in reduced-motion and accessibility contexts; a captured
intermediate counter must never look like a different business result.

## Quality evidence

Run the complete visual matrix with:

```bash
pnpm run qa:ui-quality
```

The matrix captures all distinct store-admin surfaces, public landing and
storefront, and all agency workspaces at desktop/mobile while checking viewport
overflow and serious/critical Axe violations. Flow specs then validate the
interaction states above. Broad visual changes additionally require a read-only
`agy` before/after comparison; candidates are kept only where they beat the
existing surface in product identity, color, density, hierarchy, responsive
behavior, accessibility, trust, and premium finish.

Reference libraries are inputs, not themes: [21st.dev](https://21st.dev/) for
composition, [Mobbin](https://mobbin.com/) for flow/state comparison,
[React Bits](https://reactbits.dev/) for selective interaction ideas, and
[Radix UI](https://www.radix-ui.com/primitives/docs/overview/accessibility) for
accessible behavior.
